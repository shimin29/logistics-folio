What is Logistics?

Logistics is the part of supply chain management that deals with the efficient forward and reverse flow of goods, services, and related information from the point of origin to the point of consumption according to the needs of customers, and a logistician is a professional working in the field of logistics management. Logistics management is a component that holds the supply chain together.The resources managed in logistics include physical goods such as materials, equipment, and foodstuffs, and also intangible items such as time and information.

Inventory Management in Logistics

What is Inventory Management?

A process of controlling and handling the flow of goods in a supply chain.

Why is it important?

  • Sustain the supply chain
  • Avoid high cost and disorganization
  • Improve efficiency and accuracy
  • Uses JIT, FIFO, LIFO

How it works

1. Procurement
2. Receiving
3. Recording
4. Storage
5. Monitoring
6. Fulfilment
7. Dispatch
8. Restocking

Supply Chain Security

Protecting logistics systems

Protection of goods, data, and processes from disruption across the supply chain.

Why it matters

  • Prevents theft and loss
  • Protects sensitive data
  • Ensures safe delivery
  • Reduces financial risk

How it works

Authorized access control
Tracking (GPS, RFID, barcode)
Security monitoring systems
Backup & contingency plans
